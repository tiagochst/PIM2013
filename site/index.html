Abstract

On the course of the last 20 years, many approaches have been used to try to reconstruct human faces in 3D. The most common techniques tend to use markers or active illumination on actors' faces. However, placing such markers can be time-consuming and both these techniques are extremely invasive for the actors, not to mention that they stand in the way of skin color acquisition.

Passive capture comes to try and change this scenario, by using high definition images to capture images down to pore-level geometry and reconstruct actor's performances from there. On this work, we've made an attempt to reproduce the results obtained in [Beeler 2011], using a single stereoscopic Kinect low-resolution camera to acquire performances.

However, mostly due to the camera's limitations, results are unsatisfactory.
